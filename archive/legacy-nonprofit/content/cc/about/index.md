The Airdb Foundation is a charitable fund that starts with its own capital and focuses on four programs:

- **Humanitarian relief** — emergency aid for people hit by disaster or hardship: supplies, targeted grants, and support for frontline responders.
- **Family reunification** — helping families find missing loved ones, in collaboration with reunification platforms such as [Baobeihuijia](https://www.baobeihuijia.com/).
- **Education support** — grants and living stipends for students from struggling families, starting with university students and expanding to earlier stages over time.
- **Open-source technology** — like the Linux Foundation, funding open-source projects and maintainers whose work serves the public good, starting with AI.

## How we work

**Open projects, open books.** Every project publishes its proposal and budget. Every expense is disclosed, line by line, in quarterly ledgers. When something goes wrong, we say so publicly.

**Prove it small, then open it up.** In this first phase the foundation is entirely self-funded and does not accept outside donations. Once the process holds up to scrutiny, we will open projects to volunteers and partners, and eventually — within the bounds of regulation — to public participation.

See our [transparency page](/transparency/) for the disclosure policy, ledgers, and project files, or [join us](/join/) if you'd like to take part.
